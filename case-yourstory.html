CASE STUDY 05 · BUSINESS STORYTELLING

YourStory Media

Turning founder journeys and business stories into video an everyday audience would actually watch — scripting and editing for one of India's major digital media platforms.

01 — THE BRIEF

The Brief

YourStory covers India's startup ecosystem — a subject full of jargon, funding rounds and technical products. The brief: script and edit videos that made these stories accessible without flattening what made them interesting.

02 — THE AUDIENCE

The Audience

A wide spectrum: founders and startup employees deep in the ecosystem, aspiring entrepreneurs watching for inspiration, and general viewers who want to understand the businesses changing their daily lives.

DemographicIndia's startup ecosystem plus aspiring entrepreneurs and general viewers
BehaviourWatches for both information and inspiration — the founder's story carries the business story
PlatformYourStory's digital platform and video channels
IntentLearning-led — viewers want to come away understanding something new
Pain pointsBusiness jargon and insider framing that shuts out non-specialist viewers
03 — THE STRATEGY

The Strategy

Lead with the human story, use it to carry the business story. A funding round is a headline; a founder's decision under pressure is a narrative — and narrative is what holds viewers to the end.

  • Script for retention. Every script structured so the viewer always has a reason to watch the next thirty seconds.
  • Translate, don't dumb down. Technical and financial concepts explained through analogy and example, keeping specialist viewers on side while opening the door to everyone else.
  • Explainer formats. Consistent structures that let the platform cover very different companies with a recognisable editorial voice.

06 — RESULTS

Results

Delivered a run of scripted, edited videos for one of India's most-read startup media platforms — work that proved an editorial skill now central to marketing: making complicated things watchable.

Platform view figures from this period are not available; none are estimated here.

07 — REFLECTION

Reflection

What would I improve if I ran this campaign again? I was scripting for the platform's format rather than asking whether the format fit each story. Some founder stories deserved short punchy cuts; others needed room to breathe. If I ran it again, I'd push to match format to story rather than story to format — the same instinct that later shaped how I think about platform-native content in marketing.
