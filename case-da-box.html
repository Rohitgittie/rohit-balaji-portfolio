CASE STUDY 04 · CONTENT MARKETING

Da Box

One brand voice across three surfaces — short-form video, landing-page copy and email sequencing for a subscription-box brand.

01 — THE BRIEF

The Brief

Da Box, a subscription-box brand, needed marketing content that sounded like one brand wherever a customer met it — in a social video, on a landing page, or in their inbox. The job: produce content and copy across all three, aligned to the brand's voice.

02 — THE AUDIENCE

The Audience

Subscription shoppers and gift buyers — people who buy the feeling of the unboxing as much as the products inside. They discover on social, compare on the website, and convert (or lapse) over email.

DemographicSubscription shoppers and gift buyers, discovery-driven
BehaviourDiscover on social, evaluate on the site, decide over email
PlatformSocial channels, brand website and email
IntentEmotional purchase — the unboxing moment sells the subscription
Pain pointsSubscription hesitancy — is it worth it every month, and is cancelling easy?
03 — THE STRATEGY

The Strategy

Subscription products aren't bought once — they're bought every month. So the content had to do two jobs: make the first purchase feel exciting, and make staying subscribed feel justified.

  • Voice consistency as strategy. The same tone across video, web and email, so each touchpoint reinforced the last instead of resetting the relationship.
  • Show the moment, not the products. Short-form video built around the unboxing experience — the emotional core of the purchase.
  • Copy that answers hesitation. Landing-page copy structured around the questions subscription buyers actually have before committing.
  • Email as the closer. Sequencing designed to move browsers to buyers, and buyers to repeat subscribers.
04 — THE CREATIVE

The Creative

Short-form social video, landing-page copy and email sequences — produced as one system in the brand's voice.

05 — DISTRIBUTION

Distribution

06 — RESULTS

Results

A short, focused engagement — the deliverable was a working content system across three channels in the brand's voice, handed over ready to run.

Performance metrics from this engagement were not retained; no numbers are shown rather than estimates.

07 — REFLECTION

Reflection

What would I improve if I ran this campaign again? I'd agree on measurement before producing a single asset. The work was strong, but a short engagement without tracking baked in means the story ends at "delivered" rather than "delivered and here's what it did." That gap is exactly why every project I run now starts with the question: how will we know this worked?
