ABOUT

Stories that sell
without sounding
like ads.

I started in journalism and video because I liked finding the story. Over time, the work kept moving closer to marketing: social growth, brand content, platform strategy, audience behaviour and paid campaign thinking.

That is the direction I am building now: marketing work with the clarity of journalism and the distribution awareness of social media.

By day I am at The Irish News in Belfast, leading video for Reels, TikTok and YouTube and running digital-desk shifts — writing headlines, subediting copy, building the occasional interactive graphic, and reporting some of Northern Ireland's heaviest ongoing stories.

Before journalism I built audiences: I grew a small Belfast retailer's Instagram by seventy-four thousand followers in half a year. Earlier still, I produced a fourteen-part agronomy teaching series for villages in rural Odisha. Different rooms, same job — finding the story, framing it well, and getting it in front of the right people.

Currently doing that in a new direction: Meta ads training, paid social work for Shopaire.in, and a growing focus on how editorial judgement can sharpen marketing output.

Available for marketing, content strategy, paid social and video-led projects alongside my full-time role.
