CASE STUDY 06 · EDUCATIONAL VIDEO SERIES

Asha Kiran Society

A 14-part educational video series for rural communities in Odisha — scripted, filmed, edited, animated and sound-designed end-to-end for an audience with no patience for jargon.

01 — THE BRIEF

The Brief

Asha Kiran Society needed an educational video series that rural communities in Odisha could use directly — content that taught clearly, worked for a non-specialist audience, and could be produced end-to-end by a small team.

02 — THE AUDIENCE

The Audience

Rural community members with varying levels of literacy and no background in the subject matter — an audience where the content either communicates instantly or fails completely. The most demanding audience brief on this site.

DemographicRural communities in Odisha, mixed ages and literacy levels
BehaviourGroup and community viewing rather than individual scrolling
PlatformDirect community use — screenings and shared devices, not feeds
IntentPractical learning the audience can apply in daily life
Pain pointsAbstract or text-heavy teaching excludes exactly the people the content is for
03 — THE STRATEGY

The Strategy

Design for the person furthest from the subject. If the least prepared viewer in the room understands, everyone does — so every scripting and visual decision started from that person.

  • Show before tell. Visual demonstration and animation carried the teaching; narration supported rather than led.
  • Series structure. Fourteen parts built to be watched in sequence or standalone, so communities could use them however their sessions ran.
  • Sound as accessibility. Deliberate sound design for group screenings in real-world conditions, not headphones.
  • One maker, whole pipeline. Scripting in Final Draft, filming, editing in Premiere Pro, animation in After Effects — a complete production system run end-to-end.
04 — THE CREATIVE

The Creative

Distribution

07 — REFLECTION

Reflection

What would I improve if I ran this campaign again? I'd build a feedback loop with the communities themselves — screening early cuts with real viewers before locking the series, rather than relying on the organisation's judgement of what would land. Everything I now believe about audience-first content started here: the audience is the strategy, and everything else is execution.
