CASE STUDY 02 · ORGANIC GROWTH & INFLUENCER CAMPAIGNS

Shirt in a Box

Turning a football shirt brand into a repeatable content system — 74,000 Instagram followers gained in six months, with every influencer campaign passing 100,000 views.

01 — THE BRIEF

The Brief

Shirt in a Box needed to grow its social presence organically and turn its product — football shirts — into content people actually wanted to watch, not just ads they scrolled past. The brand had a product with built-in fandom but no content system to capture it.

02 — THE AUDIENCE

The Audience

Football fans and shirt collectors — an audience that treats shirts as identity and nostalgia, not just merchandise. They live on Instagram and TikTok, engage heavily with club culture, and share content that lets them signal allegiance.

DemographicYoung football fans and kit collectors, UK and international
BehaviourHigh engagement with club culture, nostalgia and kit-reveal content; strong sharing instinct
PlatformInstagram-first, with TikTok-native short-form formats
IntentEntertainment and identity first — purchase follows affinity
Pain pointsGeneric product posts get ignored; fans respond to culture, not catalogues
03 — THE STRATEGY

The Strategy

The core insight: a product post is content once, but a format is content forever. Instead of promoting shirts, we built repeatable formats around the culture the shirts belong to — so the audience came back for the format and met the product on the way.

  • Repeatable formats over one-off posts. Content formats the audience could recognise and anticipate, making production faster and engagement compounding.
  • Influencer seeding. Campaigns built with creators whose audiences overlapped the brand's target — every campaign in the programme passed 100,000 views.
  • Systems, not sprints. Content calendars across five-plus accounts, a team of seven video editors, and CMS workflows in WordPress and Notion — so output stayed consistent as the account scaled.
  • Search-aware content. Ahrefs keyword research fed topic selection, connecting social content to what fans were actively looking for.

Growth came from the system: consistent formats, consistent cadence, and creator partnerships that put the brand inside existing fan communities rather than shouting at them from outside.

07 — REFLECTION

Reflection

What would I improve if I ran this campaign again? I'd build measurement in from day one. The growth was undeniable, but attribution was loose — I couldn't always say which format or creator drove which spike, and that made it harder to double down with confidence. Today I'd pair the same creative system with proper tracking, and I'd test paid amplification behind the formats that had already proven themselves organically.
