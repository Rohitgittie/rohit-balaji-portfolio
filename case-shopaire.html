CASE STUDY 01 · PAID SOCIAL & CREATIVE STRATEGY

Shopaire.in

Building Meta advertising that earns attention before it asks for a sale — campaign structure, creative routes, hooks and captions for an ecommerce brand.

01 — THE BRIEF

The Brief

Shopaire wanted to improve the performance of its Meta advertising while building stronger creative that encouraged engagement and conversions. The work covers ad account structure, creative direction and campaign-ready content — from hooks to captions.

02 — THE AUDIENCE

The Audience

Mobile-first ecommerce shoppers who discover products in the feed rather than searching for them. They scroll fast, judge creative in the first second, and need a reason to trust an unfamiliar brand before they buy.

DemographicOnline shoppers discovering products through social feeds
BehaviourFast scrollers — creative has one second to earn a pause
PlatformInstagram and Facebook, feed and Reels placements
IntentDiscovery-led, not search-led — the ad creates the demand
Pain pointsAd fatigue and low trust in brands they haven't bought from before
03 — THE STRATEGY

The Strategy

The approach is creative-led rather than targeting-led. Meta's delivery system rewards creative that holds attention, so the campaign structure is built to test distinct creative routes against each other instead of relying on audience segmentation to do the work.

  • Hooks first. Every creative route starts with the opening second — the hook is treated as the most valuable asset in the ad, not the offer.
  • Multiple creative routes. Distinct angles run side by side so the account learns which message earns attention, rather than guessing upfront.
  • Captions matched to intent. Copy is written for where the viewer is — discovery captions build curiosity; retargeting copy removes friction.
  • Structure that can learn. Campaigns are organised so results are readable — clean separation between prospecting and retargeting, and between creative tests.

The aim is a repeatable creative system: not one good ad, but a process that reliably produces the next one.

07 — REFLECTION

Reflection

What would I improve if I ran this campaign again? It's too early to score the campaign, but not too early to learn from it. The discipline I'm holding myself to: let the creative tests finish before drawing conclusions, and document why each route was chosen — so that when the data arrives, it confirms or kills a hypothesis rather than just being a number.
