CASE STUDY 03 · NEWSROOM CONTENT STRATEGY

The Irish News

Making serious journalism perform on platforms built for entertainment — daily packaging of complex stories for Instagram, TikTok, YouTube and search.

01 — THE BRIEF

The Brief

A Northern Ireland newsroom needs its journalism to reach audiences who no longer arrive through the front page. The ongoing challenge: take serious, often legally sensitive stories and package them for social platforms and search — without losing accuracy, clarity or trust.

02 — THE AUDIENCE

The Audience

Two audiences at once: loyal digital readers who come for depth, and younger social-first audiences who meet the journalism in a feed between entertainment content — and give it seconds, not minutes.

DemographicNorthern Ireland readers plus younger social-native audiences
BehaviourSocial audiences decide in seconds; readers expect depth and accuracy
PlatformInstagram, TikTok, YouTube, homepage and Google search
IntentA mix of active news-seeking and passive feed discovery
Pain pointsNews fatigue, distrust of sensationalism, and complex stories that resist compression
03 — THE STRATEGY

The Strategy

Every story gets packaged for the platform it will live on, not simply reposted. The same court verdict becomes a Reel, a TikTok, a homepage headline and a Google Showcase summary — each version shaped by how that audience actually consumes news.

  • Platform-native packaging. Vertical video with subtitles for social; tight, search-aware headlines for Google; homepage presentation tuned for scanning readers.
  • Compression without distortion. Headlines, captions and Showcase summaries written daily to strict character limits while preserving legal accuracy and SEO value.
  • Format range. Explainers for complex stories, data visualisations and interactive HTML graphics for investigations, Irish-language video with full subtitle formatting for underserved audiences.
  • Speed as a discipline. Newsroom deadlines force fast creative judgement — the same instinct that makes campaign creative testing decisive rather than drawn out.

This is content strategy under the hardest constraints: real-time deadlines, legal risk and an audience that punishes both dullness and sensationalism.

07 — REFLECTION

Reflection

What would I improve if I ran this campaign again? Newsroom social is often reactive — the story breaks, then the content follows. Given more room, I'd push for more planned formats: recurring series the audience returns for, rather than one-off posts chasing each news cycle. The stories that performed best were the ones where we'd already built an audience relationship, and that's a marketing lesson as much as a journalism one.
